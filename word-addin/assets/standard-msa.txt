This Master Services Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of [DATE] ("Effective Date") by and between:

[CUSTOMER NAME], a [STATE] corporation ("Customer")
and
[VENDOR NAME], a [STATE] corporation ("Vendor")

1. SERVICES AND LICENSE

1.1 License Grant. Vendor grants Customer a non-exclusive, non-transferable license to access and use the Platform for Customer's internal business purposes during the Term. The license includes access for up to the number of users specified in the applicable Order Form.

1.2 Scope of Services. Vendor shall provide the services described in the applicable Order Form, including technical support during normal business hours (9am-6pm Pacific Time, Monday-Friday, excluding holidays).

1.3 Usage Restrictions. Customer shall not: (a) sublicense, resell, or distribute the Platform; (b) reverse engineer or attempt to derive source code; (c) use the Platform for any unlawful purpose; or (d) exceed the usage limits specified in the Order Form.

2. SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS

2.1 Uptime Commitment. Vendor shall maintain Platform availability of at least 99.5% uptime per calendar month, measured on a monthly basis ("SLA").

2.2 Service Credits. If Vendor fails to meet the SLA, Customer shall be entitled to service credits as follows: 10% credit for 99.0%-99.5% uptime, 25% credit for 98.0%-99.0% uptime, and 50% credit for below 98.0% uptime.

2.3 Exclusions. SLA calculations exclude downtime resulting from: (a) scheduled maintenance with 48 hours notice; (b) Customer's acts or omissions; (c) force majeure events; or (d) third-party service failures beyond Vendor's control.

3. FEES AND PAYMENT

3.1 Fees. Customer shall pay Vendor the fees specified in the applicable Order Form.

3.2 Payment Terms. All invoices are due and payable within thirty (30) days of invoice date ("net 30"). Late payments shall accrue interest at the rate of 1.5% per month or the maximum rate permitted by law, whichever is less.

3.3 Taxes. Fees are exclusive of all applicable taxes, which Customer shall pay or reimburse to Vendor.

4. DATA PRIVACY AND SECURITY

4.1 Data Protection. Vendor shall implement and maintain appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect Customer Data against unauthorized access, loss, or disclosure, including encryption of data in transit and at rest, regular security assessments, access controls, and employee security training.

4.2 Compliance. Vendor shall comply with all applicable data protection laws, including GDPR, CCPA, and other relevant privacy regulations.

4.3 Data Breach Notification. Vendor shall notify Customer within 24 hours of becoming aware of any unauthorized access to or disclosure of Customer Data, and shall cooperate with Customer in investigating and remediating such incidents.

5. WARRANTIES

5.1 Performance Warranty. Vendor warrants that the Platform shall perform substantially in accordance with the documentation for a period of one (1) year from the Effective Date.

5.2 Professional Services. Vendor warrants that all services shall be performed in a professional and workmanlike manner consistent with industry standards.

5.3 No Infringement. Vendor warrants that the Platform does not infringe any third-party intellectual property rights.

5.4 Remedy. Customer's exclusive remedy for breach of warranty shall be, at Vendor's option: (a) correction of the non-conforming Platform; or (b) refund of fees paid for the non-conforming portion.

6. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

6.1 Liability Cap. Except for excluded claims, each party's total liability arising out of or related to this Agreement shall not exceed the greater of: (a) $100,000; or (b) the fees paid by Customer in the twelve (12) months preceding the claim.

6.2 Excluded Claims. The liability cap does not apply to: (a) either party's indemnification obligations; (b) breaches of confidentiality; (c) gross negligence or willful misconduct; or (d) violations of intellectual property rights.

6.3 Consequential Damages. Neither party shall be liable for indirect, incidental, consequential, special, or punitive damages, including lost profits, lost revenue, or lost data, even if advised of the possibility of such damages.

7. INDEMNIFICATION

7.1 Mutual Indemnification. Each party shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless the other party from third-party claims arising from: (a) breach of this Agreement; (b) negligence or willful misconduct; or (c) violation of applicable laws.

7.2 IP Indemnification. Vendor shall indemnify Customer from third-party claims that the Platform infringes intellectual property rights, provided Customer: (a) promptly notifies Vendor; (b) grants Vendor sole control of defense and settlement; and (c) reasonably cooperates in the defense.

7.3 Exceptions. Vendor's IP indemnification does not apply to claims arising from: (a) Customer's modifications to the Platform; (b) combination with non-Vendor products; or (c) Customer's continued use after Vendor provides a non-infringing alternative.

8. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

8.1 Platform Ownership. Vendor retains all right, title, and interest in the Platform and related intellectual property.

8.2 Customer Data Ownership. Customer retains all right, title, and interest in Customer Data. Customer grants Vendor a limited license to use Customer Data solely to provide the services.

8.3 Feedback. Customer may provide suggestions or feedback regarding the Platform. Vendor may use such feedback without obligation to Customer.

9. TERM AND TERMINATION

9.1 Initial Term. This Agreement commences on the Effective Date and continues for an initial term of twelve (12) months.

9.2 Renewal. This Agreement shall automatically renew for successive twelve (12) month periods unless either party provides written notice of non-renewal at least ninety (90) days before the end of the then-current term.

9.3 Termination for Convenience. Either party may terminate this Agreement for convenience upon ninety (90) days' written notice.

9.4 Termination for Cause. Either party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other party: (a) materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure within thirty (30) days of written notice; or (b) becomes insolvent or subject to bankruptcy proceedings.

9.5 Effect of Termination. Upon termination: (a) all licenses granted hereunder immediately terminate; (b) Customer shall cease using the Platform; (c) Vendor shall return or destroy Customer Data within thirty (30) days; and (d) Customer shall pay all fees accrued through the termination date.

10. GENERAL PROVISIONS

10.1 Governing Law. This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of California, without regard to conflict of law principles.

10.2 Dispute Resolution. Any disputes shall be resolved through binding arbitration in San Francisco, California, under the rules of the American Arbitration Association.

10.3 Amendments. This Agreement may be modified only by written amendment signed by both parties.

10.4 Assignment. Neither party may assign this Agreement without the other party's prior written consent, except to a successor in connection with a merger or acquisition.

10.5 Severability. If any provision is found invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect.
